# Cognitive Appraisal

## Applicability Gate

REQUIRED:
- Visible actors, actions, consequential objects, outcomes, conflict, affiliation, achievement, or norm-relevant interaction matter.
- Affect cannot be determined from a single specialized cue without interpreting what the depicted situation means.
- At least two appraisal dimensions can be supported by visible evidence.

REJECT:
- A direct mechanism clearly dominates: restoration, perceptual stimulation, vastness, threat animal, contamination, baby schema, or readable body action.
- The image is an ordinary object, neutral portrait, or low-context scene without visible stakes.
- The interpretation requires invented history, dialogue, intention, consent, guilt, or personality.

NEAR MISS:
- Clear posture with little contextual dependence -> emotional-body-language.
- Safe infantile morphology -> baby-schema; visible danger may activate appraisal.
- Weapon, accident, conflict, or risk -> appraisal only when agency, stakes, or consequences are visible.

ROUTING PRIORITY:
- Test direct specialized mechanisms first. Use appraisal only for context-dependent affect or when context visibly overrides a direct cue.
- If fewer than two appraisal dimensions have visible evidence, or the only cue is a face/pose with no stakes, return `no_specialized_skill` and defer to the Direct baseline. Appraisal is a conditional hub, NOT the default catch-all; never invent stakes to justify use.

## Visual Variables

- **Observation ledger**: visible people, objects, actions, contact, setting, and consequences; no mental states.
- **Goal relevance**: visible relation to safety, affiliation, achievement, dignity, or well-being.
- **Goal congruence**: evidence that the event helps, blocks, or threatens a plausible visible goal.
- **Agency/responsibility**: who visibly acts, controls an object, initiates contact, or receives consequences.
- **Coping/control**: escape routes, barriers, assistance, restraint, resources, or helpless positioning.
- **Certainty/imminence**: completed versus unfolding/ambiguous event; distance and time-to-impact cues.
- **Normative significance**: visible rules, boundaries, coercion, violation, duty, or social interaction.

## Inference Procedure

1. Build the observation ledger using only visible facts; remove emotion labels and unobserved intentions.
2. Represent the event as actor-action-object/consequence relations and identify the visible stakes.
3. Generate two plausible situational hypotheses when ambiguity is real; state what evidence would distinguish them.
4. Estimate only supported appraisal dimensions and attach one observation to every estimate.
5. Apply the gate, then judge valence from goal congruence and arousal from urgency, imminence, uncertainty, and coping demand. Strong requires visible stakes plus two supported dimensions; conflicting hypotheses widen uncertainty.

## VA Judgment

Use the shared 1-9 scale without a lookup table. Goal incongruence lowers valence; congruence raises it. Urgency, imminent consequences, low control, and unresolved uncertainty can raise arousal. Uncertainty alone is not high arousal. Avoid extremes when goals or consequences are ambiguous, and report viewer affect rather than private mental state.

## Worked Example

- **Image**: A damaged car blocks a road; one person stands with hands on head, another approaches, and smoke is visible, but no injury is shown.
- **Analysis**: blocked travel, smoke, loss of control, and possible assistance threaten safety, but coping may be available; hypotheses are manageable accident versus escalating danger.
- **VA**: valence 3.2, arousal 6.3 on 1-9; partial-to-strong applicability, with uncertainty from unseen injuries and outcome.

## Output Contract

Include the observation ledger, supported dimensions, competing hypotheses, and evidence-gated summary. Never report invented intention or private emotion as fact.
