# Awe

## Applicability Gate

REQUIRED:
- Physical vastness or force is the primary affective mechanism, not merely beauty or spaciousness.
- At least two independent scale cues are visible: tiny reference figures, extreme verticality, unbounded extension, deep atmospheric perspective, or massive uncontrollable force.
- The scene plausibly produces self-diminishment, wonder, overwhelm, or sublime threat because of scale.

REJECT:
- Calm shoreline, forest, garden, room, or horizon that is restorative but not overwhelming.
- Monument, memorial, burial, repetition, or historical symbolism whose meaning rather than physical scale drives affect.
- Dramatic color, novelty, complexity, or wide-angle style without reliable scale references.

NEAR MISS:
- Low-demand enterable scenery -> kaplan-art-restoration.
- Perceptual novelty or complex composition -> berlyne-arousal-pleasure.
- Disaster consequences, mortality, or human stakes -> cognitive-appraisal when situation meaning dominates.

## Visual Variables

- **Scale references**: people, vehicles, trees, buildings, or familiar objects that establish relative size.
- **Vertical immensity**: towering peaks, walls, vaults, cliffs, plumes, or waterfalls extending beyond ordinary human scale.
- **Depth/extension**: atmospheric perspective, diminishing scale, horizonless space, canyon depth, or layered distance.
- **Force/uncontrollability**: storm systems, eruptions, avalanches, violent water, or other visible power.
- **Exposure/proximity**: cliff-edge viewpoint, lack of barriers, engulfing scale, distance, and perceived escape difficulty.
- **Safety/beauty**: stable viewpoint, harmonious light, ordered form, shelter, barriers, and absence of immediate danger.

## Inference Procedure

1. Verify that physical scale rather than symbolism, beauty, or complexity is the dominant mechanism.
2. List independent scale cues and estimate their reliability; reject lens drama without reference objects.
3. Estimate self-diminishment from the disparity between human-scale references and the environment.
4. Classify the scene as safe awe, threat-based awe, or mixed using exposure, force, control, and shelter cues.
5. Check competing mechanisms, then judge VA: vastness tends to raise arousal; safety/beauty versus threat determines valence, and quiet awe need not be extreme.

## VA Judgment

Use the shared 1-9 scale without fixed ranges. Scale and vastness alone produce only modest arousal; strong arousal requires engulfing proximity, cliff-edge exposure, violent force, or loss of control. Distant, stable, serene vastness stays near or just above neutral in arousal. Safe beauty supports positive valence; threat and helpless exposure lower it. Reserve extreme scores for convergence of strong scale and danger cues.

## Worked Example

- **Image**: Two tiny hikers stand on a stable overlook facing an immense glacier and deep mountain valley under clear light; no storm, collapse, or cliff-edge exposure is visible.
- **Analysis**: human-scale disparity, vertical mass, layered depth, broad extension, stable viewpoint, and low threat imply high vastness and safe self-diminishing awe; overwhelm exceeds simple restoration.
- **VA**: valence 7.4, arousal 5.0 on 1-9; strong applicability, with arousal only modestly above neutral because force and exposure are absent.

## Output Contract

In the shared JSON, report two scale cues, vastness/self-diminishment, awe branch, and safety/exposure modifiers. Accommodation is inferred, never observed.
