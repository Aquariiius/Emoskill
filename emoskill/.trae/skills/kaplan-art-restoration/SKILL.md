# Kaplan Attention Restoration Theory

## Applicability Gate

REQUIRED:
- An enterable or inhabitable environment is the dominant affective source.
- Visible spatial organization supports low-demand viewing, gentle exploration, rest, or attentional recovery.
- At least three restorative variables are observable, including one spatial variable.

REJECT:
- Isolated object, product, texture, tabletop arrangement, or decorative still life.
- Overwhelming scale, vertigo, violent force, dense novelty, threat, or person-centered action dominates.
- Calmness comes mainly from blankness or lack of content rather than environmental structure.

NEAR MISS:
- Immense scale and self-diminishment -> awe.
- Novelty, ambiguity, dense pattern, or hard fascination -> berlyne-arousal-pleasure.
- Human stakes or visible danger -> cognitive-appraisal.

## Visual Variables

- **Apparent openness**: sky, horizon, lateral span, enclosure, and obstruction; never claim physical meters.
- **Spatial depth**: foreground-middle-background layers, occlusion, linear perspective, scale reduction, paths, and vanishing structure.
- **Enterability/affordance**: paths, seating, shelter, walkable surfaces, accessible edges, or usable interiors.
- **Coherence/extent**: connected layout, legible regions, continuity, and enough visual world to explore mentally.
- **Soft fascination**: water, foliage, clouds, reflections, gentle light, or repeating natural detail that holds attention without demanding search.
- **Compatibility/refuge**: fit with resting, strolling, observing, or sheltering; prospect-refuge balance.
- **Attentional demand**: clutter, abrupt contrast, ambiguity, crowding, obstacles, warning cues, and threat.

## Inference Procedure

1. Confirm that an environment, not an object or person, carries the affect and assess enterability.
2. Estimate apparent openness and depth from named monocular cues; do not invent physical dimensions.
3. Evaluate coherence/extent and compatibility from layout and visible affordances.
4. Separate soft fascination from Berlyne-style hard fascination, and record clutter, threat, or spectacle that consumes attention.
5. Integrate evidence into restorative potential and judge VA: restoration supports positive valence and low-to-moderate arousal; stimulation, exposure, or threat weakens the pattern.

## VA Judgment

Use the shared 1-9 scale without fixed ranges. Raise valence only when the environment appears safe, coherent, and compatible with low-demand activity. Lower arousal when depth, legibility, refuge, and soft fascination support recovery. Do not equate visual detail with arousal or force every natural scene toward calmness. Strong drama, exposure, crowding, or danger can keep arousal moderate/high and reduce applicability.

## Worked Example

- **Image**: A lakeside path curves through trees toward an open view of water and distant hills under soft daylight; no people, warnings, or obstacles are prominent.
- **Analysis**: clear depth layers, walkable path, open prospect, shaded refuge, coherent layout, gentle natural detail, and low clutter support strong extent, compatibility, soft fascination, and low demand; no awe or threat override.
- **VA**: valence 7.3, arousal 3.2 on 1-9; strong applicability with minor uncertainty about viewer preference.

## Output Contract

In the shared JSON, report spatial cues, ART constructs, restorative potential, and competing stimulation or threat. Do not claim measured dimensions, safety, or actual recovery.
